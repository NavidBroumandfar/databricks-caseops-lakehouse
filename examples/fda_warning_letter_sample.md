Dear Mr. Holloway,

The United States Food and Drug Administration (FDA) inspected your drug manufacturing
facility at 4800 Industrial Parkway, Suite 200, Springfield, IL 62704 during the period of
January 8 through January 22, 2025. This inspection was conducted by investigators from
the FDA Chicago District Office.

Based on our inspection findings, we have determined that your facility is in significant
violation of Current Good Manufacturing Practice (CGMP) regulations for finished
pharmaceuticals as set forth in Title 21, Code of Federal Regulations (CFR), Parts 210
and 211.

---

## Violations Observed

### Violation 1 — Failure to Establish Adequate Laboratory Controls (21 CFR § 211.160(b))

Your firm failed to establish scientifically sound and appropriate specifications, standards,
sampling plans, and test procedures designed to ensure that drug products conform to
established standards of identity, strength, quality, and purity. Specifically:

- Lot #MPS-24-0891 (Amoxicillin Trihydrate Capsules, 500 mg) was released for distribution
  without completed dissolution testing results. Release records indicated the testing was
  "in progress" at the time of release.
- Your analytical method for sterility testing had not been validated for the current
  product matrix. The validation report referenced a different formulation and predated
  current batch composition changes made in November 2023.

### Violation 2 — Inadequate Investigation of Out-of-Specification (OOS) Results (21 CFR § 211.192)

Your firm failed to conduct thorough investigations when out-of-specification results were
identified. Our investigators reviewed 14 OOS events documented in calendar year 2024. Of
these, 9 were closed as "analyst error" without supporting documentation establishing a
root cause or demonstrating the analyst error occurred. This pattern is inconsistent with
the requirements of a scientifically sound OOS investigation.

### Violation 3 — Failure to Maintain Adequate Equipment Qualification Records (21 CFR § 211.68)

Automatic, mechanical, and electronic equipment was not routinely calibrated, inspected,
or checked according to a written program designed to assure proper performance. Our
inspection found that three HPLC units in Laboratory B had not received annual qualification
assessments since 2022, despite being used for batch release testing throughout 2023 and 2024.

---

## Corrective Actions Required

FDA requests that you take prompt action to correct these violations. Within **fifteen (15)
working days** of receipt of this letter, you must:

1. Provide a written response to this Warning Letter describing the specific steps you have
   taken, or plan to take, to bring your operations into compliance with the applicable
   regulations.

2. Submit a Corrective Action and Preventive Action (CAPA) plan that:
   - Identifies the root cause of each cited violation
   - Describes interim controls implemented to prevent recurrence
   - Provides a timeline for full remediation with milestones

3. Conduct a retrospective review of all product released to market in the 24 months
   preceding this letter that was subject to the laboratory control deficiencies identified
   in Violation 1, and report findings to this office.

Failure to promptly correct these violations may result in regulatory action without further
notice. Such action may include seizure and/or injunction. Additionally, your firm's drug
products are subject to refusal of admission into the United States if those products appear
to be adulterated.
